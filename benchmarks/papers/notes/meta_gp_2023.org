#+TITLE: Meta-GP Conformer Exploration 2023 Notes

* Role In The Literature

This paper is about exhaustive torsional conformer exploration via
physical-prior-mean-driven /meta-Gaussian processes/.
It is not about deep GPs or neural-network GP hybrids.
The central idea is cross-task reuse: surrogate PESs learned while optimizing
earlier conformers become candidate prior mean functions for later conformers.

* Method Shape

- The paper defines a geometry meta-optimization workflow over a sequence of
  conformer optimizations.
- In the meta-GP setting, the optimizer keeps an augmented dataset and inherits
  surrogate PESs from previous conformer optimizations as candidate priors for
  the next task.
- The prior-selection step is adaptive: the optimizer switches to the “best”
  prior using a force/gradient-matching criterion.
- The method therefore combines two ingredients:
  1. physical nonconstant priors from lower-level ab initio models;
  2. recycled local surrogate PESs from previous optimizations.

* Domain And Benchmarks

- The target domain is torsional conformer search, especially amino-acid-style
  molecules with multiple internal rotations.
- The paper studies efficiency, number of unique conformers found, and the
  quality of downstream thermodynamic quantities.
- The benchmark logic is not “find one minimum faster,” but “explore a large
  torsional space more completely and more cheaply across many related
  optimization tasks.”

* What Makes It A Meta-GP

- The paper explicitly contrasts ordinary GP optimization against meta-GP
  optimization.
- The meta aspect is the transfer of /learned local PES objects/ between tasks,
  not a deeper network architecture.
- Previous surrogate PESs are treated as prior mean candidates, so the GP is
  learning a residual on top of prior local approximations gathered from the
  same molecule’s conformer space.
- This is a hierarchy across optimization tasks, not just a richer prior within
  one task.

* What Matters For ChemGP

- This paper is the correct citation for /recycled local PES priors/ and
  hierarchy-style meta-GP conformer search.
- It should not be collapsed into the same box as fixed physical priors for
  one-shot minimization or CI-NEB.
- The directly portable idea for ChemGP is a prior-mean interface that can
  accept previously trained local surrogates as candidate priors.
- A faithful reproduction would require task sequencing, surrogate persistence,
  and prior selection across related optimization runs, which is larger than the
  minimal tutorial benchmark harness.
- For the manuscript, the key point is that conformer-space exploration is a
  distinct use case where cross-task reuse of local PES information becomes the
  main methodological contribution.
