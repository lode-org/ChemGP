#+TITLE: Physical Prior Mean CI-NEB 2024 Notes

* Role In The Literature

This paper is a CI-NEB optimizer paper, not a general GP tutorial and not a
deep-learning GP paper.
Its core claim is that the GP prior mean should be a /nonconstant physical
function/ that already approximates the global PES coarsely, rather than a
constant or maximum-energy offset.
The target problem is minimum-energy-path and transition-state optimization with
CI-NEB across gas-phase, interfacial, and bulk-phase reactions.

* Method Shape

- GP regression is built around a physical prior mean \(\mu(x)\), so the GP fits
  the residual relative to that prior instead of learning from a constant
  baseline.
- The optimizer uses a target-image global-surrogate update-and-search cycle:
  one image is selected by surrogate uncertainty, evaluated on the true PES, the
  global surrogate is updated, and then an AIE-style surrogate CI-NEB
  relaxation is performed on the surrogate PES.
- Image targeting is variance-driven. The paper defines the target image from
  the largest component of the variance vector, then updates that image on the
  true PES.
- The method still optimizes the whole MEP, not just the saddle image, and it
  states that the same force convergence criteria are enforced for all images.

* Prior Mean Construction

- The prior mean is explicitly physical and nonconstant.
- For surface chemistry cases, the paper proposes a hybrid energy-function prior
  by partitioning the system into reaction-cluster and environment pieces.
- The environment is modeled with EMT for supported metals; the reaction cluster
  is modeled with DFTB3; an LJ term supplies the cross interaction.
- The paper emphasizes that the prior need not be quantitatively exact: it is a
  physically guided first approximation that points the surrogate search in the
  right region of the PES.

* Benchmarks And Metrics

- The benchmark emphasis is efficiency relative to standard nonsurrogate CI-NEB
  optimizers and relative to constant-prior GP baselines.
- Reported metrics include total SCF calls and failed-convergence cases.
- The paper explicitly compares a physical-prior GP against a constant-prior GP
  and classical optimizers such as FIRE and L-BFGS.
- The systems span Baker-TS-style molecular reactions, ion migration in
  MOFs, and Cu(111) interfacial reactions.

* What Matters For ChemGP

- This paper is the correct citation for /physical prior mean CI-NEB/.
- It is not the same method family as the zero-mean plus constant-offset local
  GP taught in the main tutorial manuscript.
- The directly comparable concept for ChemGP is: one-image CI-NEB acquisition
  with a physically informed nonconstant prior mean.
- The paper’s hybrid EMT+DFTB3+LJ prior is domain-specific and should be cited
  as an example of physical prior construction, not treated as the only valid
  form.
- For the manuscript, the key pedagogical takeaway is that CI-NEB can be
  accelerated either by changing the acquisition/update logic, by changing the
  prior mean, or by doing both.
