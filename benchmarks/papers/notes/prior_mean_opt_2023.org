#+TITLE: Adaptive Prior Mean Geometry Optimization 2023 Notes

* Role In The Literature

This paper is about molecular geometry optimization via
gradient-enhanced universal kriging (GEUK) with /adaptive ab initio prior mean
functions/ in curvilinear coordinates.
It is not a generic fixed-prior GP paper.
Its main contribution is the combination of:

- nonconstant ab initio prior means,
- on-the-fly switching between candidate priors,
- optimization in curvilinear/internal-coordinate representations.

* Method Shape

- The regression model is GEUK rather than ordinary kriging: the prior mean is
  nonconstant and physically motivated.
- The adaptive step is central. The optimizer switches among candidate prior
  mean functions during the optimization rather than fixing one prior for the
  whole run.
- The prior-selection logic is based on gradient matching between the target QM
  gradient and the prior-mean gradient at the current geometry.
- The paper distinguishes an incomplete curvilinear scheme from a complete
  curvilinear scheme; the complete scheme fits and minimizes in curvilinear
  coordinates, while the incomplete scheme still minimizes through Cartesian
  coordinates.

* Coordinate And Transformation Content

- Curvilinear coordinates are a first-class part of the method, not an
  implementation detail.
- The paper spends substantial effort on the algebra of the B matrix,
  pseudo-inverse transformations, and iterative back transformation from
  internal to Cartesian coordinates.
- Redundant internal, delocalized internal, hybrid delocalized internal
  Z-matrix, and redundant Coulombic coordinates are all discussed.
- The complete-curvilinear approach is preferred for the adaptive-prior GEUK
  setting.

* Benchmarks And Metrics

- The paper benchmarks on ext-Baker-style geometry sets and on more challenging
  systems such as Criegee intermediates.
- Performance is discussed mainly in optimization steps, not only wall time.
- The comparisons are against constant-prior kriging and standard non-surrogate
  geometry optimizers.
- The paper concludes that internal/curvilinear coordinates still help, but the
  advantage over Cartesian coordinates becomes weaker once informative adaptive
  priors are used.

* What Matters For ChemGP

- This paper is the correct citation for /adaptive prior selection in geometry
  optimization/.
- Its key conceptual lesson is that prior quality and coordinate choice are
  coupled: if the prior is physical and adaptive, the optimizer should also be
  clear about what coordinate system the surrogate lives in.
- A faithful reproduction in ChemGP would require more than a prior hook; it
  would need an optimizer-side curvilinear-coordinate layer and transformation
  machinery.
- For the tutorial manuscript, this work should be presented as a neighboring
  extension of surrogate geometry optimization, not as the same formulation as
  the local inverse-distance Cartesian tutorial code.
