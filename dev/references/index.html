References

Key publications underlying the methods implemented in ChemGP.

GP-Guided Optimization

  • Denzel, A. & Kastner, J. (2018). Gaussian process regression for geometry optimization. J. Chem. Phys., 148, 094114. doi:10.1063/1.5017103

  • Denzel, A. & Kastner, J. (2018). Gaussian process regression for transition state search. J. Chem. Theory Comput., 14, 5777–5786. doi:10.1021/acs.jctc.8b00708

GP-Dimer Method

  • Koistinen, O.-P., Maras, E., Vehtari, A. & Jonsson, H. (2020). Minimum mode saddle point searches using Gaussian process regression with inverse-distance covariance function. J. Chem. Theory Comput., 16, 499–509. doi:10.1021/acs.jctc.9b01038

Dimer Method

  • Henkelman, G. & Jonsson, H. (1999). A dimer method for finding saddle points on high dimensional potential surfaces using only first derivatives. J. Chem. Phys., 111, 7010–7022. doi:10.1063/1.480097

Gaussian Process Regression

  • Rasmussen, C. E. & Williams, C. K. I. (2006). Gaussian Processes for Machine Learning. MIT Press. www.gaussianprocess.org/gpml

  • Solak, E., Murray-Smith, R., Leithead, W. E., Leith, D. J. & Rasmussen, C. E. (2003). Derivative observations in Gaussian process models of dynamic systems. Advances in Neural Information Processing Systems, 15.

Inverse Distance Features

  • Koistinen, O.-P., Dagbjartsdottir, F. B., Asgeirsson, V., Vehtari, A. & Jonsson, H. (2017). Nudged elastic band calculations accelerated with Gaussian process regression. J. Chem. Phys., 147, 152720. doi:10.1063/1.4986787
