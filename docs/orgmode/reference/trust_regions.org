* Trust Regions

Trust regions constrain GP-guided steps to regions where the surrogate has
reliable predictions. Without trust constraints, the optimizer can step into
regions far from any training data, where GP predictions are unreliable and can
produce arbitrarily wrong energies and gradients.

** Distance Metrics

ChemGP supports two trust distance metrics, selected via =TrustMetric=:

*** EMD (Earth Mover's Distance)

The Earth Mover's Distance between the inverse-distance features of a proposed
point and the nearest training point. This is the natural metric for molecular
systems because it operates in the invariant feature space. A large EMD means
the molecular geometry is structurally different from anything in the training
set.

Implementation: brute-force assignment over sorted feature vectors. Exact for
1D feature comparisons (which is the case for comparing individual pair-type
distance vectors).

*** Euclidean

Simple Euclidean distance in coordinate space. Use for non-molecular surfaces
(Muller-Brown, analytical potentials) where there are no interatomic distances
to compute.

** Trust Clipping

When the proposed step would place the new point beyond =trust_radius= from the
nearest training point (measured by the chosen metric), the step is clipped to
stay within the trust region. The direction is preserved; only the magnitude is
reduced.

** 1D Max-Log Distance

A faster alternative to full EMD: computes the maximum absolute difference in
log-space features across all dimensions. Used for FPS subset selection and
coarse trust screening, where the exact distance is less critical than ranking.

** Adaptive Trust Threshold (OTGPD)

The OTGPD method uses an adaptive GP trust threshold instead of a fixed trust
radius:

#+begin_example
T_gp = max(min(F_true) / divisor, T_dimer / 10)
#+end_example

This tightens as the optimizer approaches convergence, trusting the GP more when
forces are already small. When the GP-predicted force is below =T_gp=, the GP is
trusted and no oracle call is made.

** Trust Region Penalty (Minimization)

The inner L-BFGS for minimization uses a soft penalty rather than hard clipping:

#+begin_example
penalty = penalty_coeff * max(0, dist - trust_radius)^2
#+end_example

This adds a quadratic penalty to the objective when the optimizer ventures
outside the trust region, gently steering it back without introducing
discontinuities in the gradient.
